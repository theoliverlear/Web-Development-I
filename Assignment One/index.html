Painting

A painting which I will always hang on my wall is a painting of a an album cover from the music I made. It was called "Hand-Me-Downs" and this painting reminds me of what everyone in my life did to help me through a difficult time. To not be specific, the trauma of my childhood made me very unwell. I was relentlessly bullied my whole time in public school. It led me to leaving my school junior year of high school. My one real friend, who I had known since I was 6, was the only person I had to talk to. I was so lonely and depressed. Over the course of these years, we would play tennis together, watch movies, and talk. He's an introvert, and I'm an extrovert, so I knew that when said yes to hanging out, that it was a big deal. I was so grateful for him and others in my family who loved me as I was. So this painting by my sister, who is a shy painter but said yes to painting for me, reminds me of the people who were there for me when I needed them most.

Library

My library holds a special place in my heart. In September of 2022, when I started my programming journey, I was so excited to learn. Not knowing what resources were out there, I stumbled around websites, mostly forums like StackOverflow and found a lot of non-answers without any context. I learned this way, but it was difficult and slow. I was reminded of an old adage, "you get what you pay for." I decided to buy a book on Java and it was a game changer. I was able to learn so much more quickly! I had examples, context, and a clear, almost sequential path to follow. Since that point, I have bought many books on programming, cybersecurity, operating systems, and more. As I continue to learn, I gather more books in my library. You can scroll through them in the slideshow to the right. People often wonder how I learned so much so quickly since I started programming in September of 2022. I attest to them that I am not special, I just found the right resources. I am grateful for my library and the knowledge it has given me. I continue to look forward to learning more and I have my books to thank for my past, present, and future learning.

Computer

As a developer, something meaningful to me is my computer. I ended up being able to afford it after trading cryptocurrencies in early 2021. I was particularly proud of myself for being able to afford it because I had been only been able to use weak laptops or cheap desktops for my entire life. I had always wanted a powerful computer and I finally had the means to get one from working hard and making smart trades. In summer of 2021, I fully assembled my computer — which had all the items I'd hoped for. It is a self-built computer and it was initially intended for editing audio as my current job was working as an audio engineer. I also used to to produce my own music with the computer. I have since moved on to software development and I use it for that now. I am very proud of my computer and the things I can do with it. I'll never forget the first Codecademy class I completed with it. I was so excited to be learning something new and I'm so grateful and lucky that I had a computer to do it on. My computer is a manifestation of my personality where I can create and learn new things by myself — on my own accord.

Security Items

In August 2022, I was hacked. As a freelance audio engineer, I came across a file that ended up being malicious. It started with my computer being hacked and spread to any other device I had on my network. Accounts associated with me were hacked. I was devastated and felt powerless — nothing prepared me for this. Only those who have been hacked know how scary it is. Bank accounts, social media, private messages, and more were all exposed. My phone calls would be redirected, my texts would be read, and my computer I worked so hard to afford was now a tool for the hacker — mostly to mine cryptocurrency. I was scared and it took me awhile to accept the fact that I could not solve it with half-measures. After months, I had to wipe my computer, phone, laptop, anything that was connected to my initial infiltration. This was particularly difficult because I had to wipe my computer which held all my music projects. I had to wipe, what where my dreams, into nothing. This was a very difficult time and realizing time for me. I learned a lot about the technology field. It, along with the devastation of my music library, that was the catalyst for me to switch my life path from music to technology. Show more

I am grateful for the experience and I am grateful for the lessons I learned. The items shown are the pieces of technology which gave me my life back after a months' long battle with the hacker. The first is a YubiKey which is a hardware security key. It's a physical key that I can use to authenticate my accounts. It's the most secure way to protect myself because only the key holder can access the account. The second item is my heavy-duty router. This router is an absolute beast and it is the most secure router I could find. It has a built-in firewall, VPN, and it is very difficult to hack. It sustained at least a half dozen DDOS attacks and I believe after handling each one, the hacker gave up. Though costly, it was worth it and I am very grateful for the freedom it has given me. These items are a daily reminder of one of the most important events of my life and how responding to situations with full-measures can be painful, but a necessary pain to solve a problem.
